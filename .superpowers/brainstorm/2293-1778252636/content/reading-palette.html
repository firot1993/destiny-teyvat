Scene I

The bell at Mondstadt's cathedral struck six as Lirien crossed the square. She had not meant to come here. The wind had brought her — or someone in it had — and now the cobbles still held the warmth of an afternoon she'd missed.

A figure waited at the cathedral steps. Cloaked, unmoving. Her Vision pulsed once at her hip, three quick beats, like a heart not her own.

— Approach the figure, slowly
— Wait for them to turn first
— Slip past, into the cathedral

Scene II — long version

The figure spoke first, before Lirien could. The voice was older than the face under the hood, older than Mondstadt's bells, and it knew her — not as a stranger knows a name, but as the wind knows a path it has walked many times.

"You're late, wind-walker. The lake won't wait twice."

Lirien's Vision pulsed again, harder this time, three beats and a fourth. The fourth one stayed. She had read about Visions that did this — old Visions, ones that had belonged to someone before, and remembered them. She had never wanted to wear one of those.

The figure turned, just enough that moonlight caught a strand of pale hair, and a cheekbone that looked far too young for the voice it wore. They lifted one hand, palm up, in invitation. The air above the palm shimmered, the way it did before a wind opened a door no one had built.

"You can come, or you can stay. The lake is patient about most things, but it has stopped being patient about you." A pause, gentler. "I have been waiting a long time for someone to say my name back to me. You're the closest anyone has come."

Behind Lirien, the cathedral bells began to strike seven, and would not stop until they had finished. She had perhaps the length of seven bells to decide whether the wind that brought her here had been her own.

— Ask whose voice that really is
— Follow them toward the lake without a word
— Refuse — turn back to the city
