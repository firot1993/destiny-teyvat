Three candidate arcs for scene-number color/light

Each strip shows the background at scenes 1 → 10. Click the curve that best matches the arc you want.

Curve A — Literal day cycle

Page tracks a single day: dawn → noon → dusk → night → dawn. Reads as time passing. Goes very dark in the middle (panels 7–9 invert the parchment to deep night), so legibility flips. Strongest "passage" feel; most expensive on the editorial mood.

Curve B — Emotional storyboard

Palette tracks tension, not time. Warm parchment opens, cools through 2–4, warms again at 5 (false peace), storms in 6–7, ember-red climax at 8, ash at 9, cool dawn-blue at 10. Most dramatic. Mid-arc panels still invert legibility; you'd need a typography pass to keep prose readable on dark grounds.

Curve C — Light-source vignette only

Parchment ground stays. A radial light glow drifts across the page — top-left dawn → overhead noon → low-right dusk → bottom night-glow → returning dawn. Same prose readability everywhere. Subtler. Reads as light moving across a single room rather than the world changing.
