She did not move. She let the silence at her own throat answer first, and after a long while the figure inclined its head, as if it had been waiting on exactly that. The cloak shifted; one hand emerged, ungloved, palm up.

"Most come at the wind," they said. "You came at the quiet. Good." The voice was older than the face it half-revealed, and Lirien's Vision answered it before her tongue did — three pulses, a fourth that lingered.

"Walk with me. I'll explain the lake."
